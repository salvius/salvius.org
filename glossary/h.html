Glossary - H

A glossary of terms commonly used in robotics, electronics, and programming.

Back to Glossary Index

H

Haptic
Tactile feedback technology using the operator's sense of touch. Also sometimes applied to robot manipulators with their own touch sensitivity.
H-Bridge
An electronic circuit that enables a voltage to be applied across a load in either direction. H-bridges are commonly used in robotics to allow DC motors to run forwards or backwards.
Header pins
Small metal pins used to connect electronic components. Headers can be plug pins or sockets to receive the plugs.
Heat sink
A passive heat exchanger that transfers the heat generated by an electronic or mechanical device to a fluid medium, often air or a liquid coolant, where it is dissipated away from the device, thereby allowing regulation of the device's temperature at optimal levels.
Heat shrink tubing
Polymer tubing which contracts when heated. Heat shrink tubing is typically used to insulate wire splices.
Hexapod (platform)
A movable platform using six linear actuators. Often used in flight simulators and fairground rides, they also have applications as a robotic manipulator.
Hexapod (walker)
A six-legged walking robot, using a simple insect-like locomotion.
Humanoid
A robotic entity designed to resemble a human being in form, function, or both.
Hydraulics
The control of mechanical force and movement, generated by the application of liquid under pressure.
